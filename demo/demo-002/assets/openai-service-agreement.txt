1. GOVERNING LAW AND JURISDICTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1.1 These Terms are governed by the laws of the State of California, USA,
    excluding its conflicts-of-laws principles.

1.2 All disputes shall be resolved exclusively in the federal or state courts
    of San Francisco, California, USA.

    [CARLSBERG LEGAL NOTE — HIGH RISK]: California governing law is
    non-standard for a Danish company. Carlsberg's standard vendor agreements
    use Danish law and Copenhagen jurisdiction. This creates:
    (a) Significant litigation cost and access-to-justice risk for Carlsberg.
    (b) Potential conflict with mandatory EU consumer/B2B protection rules.
    (c) Enforceability risk under Danish contract law for certain clauses.
    STATUS: Must escalate to Group General Counsel. Non-negotiable for OpenAI
    per their standard terms. Consider risk acceptance with legal sign-off.

1.3 Dispute Resolution: Mandatory binding arbitration through NAM (National
    Arbitration and Mediation), San Francisco-based.

    [CARLSBERG LEGAL NOTE — MEDIUM RISK]: Class action waiver and mandatory
    arbitration may limit Carlsberg's legal remedies in a dispute scenario.
    Batch arbitration provisions apply if 25+ claimants file simultaneously.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. DATA PROCESSING AND GDPR COMPLIANCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

2.1 OpenAI acts as Data Processor under GDPR Article 28 for Customer Data
    submitted via the API and Enterprise services.

2.2 OpenAI Ireland Ltd. is the contracting entity for EEA customers.
    EEA and Swiss data is processed under SCCs (Standard Contractual Clauses)
    per EU Commission Decision of June 4, 2021.

    [CARLSBERG LEGAL NOTE — POSITIVE]: This is the correct GDPR structure.
    OpenAI Ireland Ltd. as data processor with SCCs provides an adequate
    legal transfer mechanism for Carlsberg's EU customer data.

2.3 Sub-processors: OpenAI uses sub-processors listed at
    https://platform.openai.com/subprocessors
    Customer receives 30 days notice of changes and may object.

    [CARLSBERG LEGAL NOTE — MEDIUM RISK]: Sub-processor list includes
    US-based cloud infrastructure (Microsoft Azure, AWS). While SCCs cover
    the transfer mechanism, US data localisation risk remains under
    US CLOUD Act — US government may compel access to data held by US companies
    even on EU infrastructure. This requires DPIA (Data Protection Impact
    Assessment) before go-live.
    STATUS: DPIA required. IT Security and DPO sign-off needed.

2.4 Data Retention: OpenAI will return or delete Customer Data following
    expiry or termination of the Agreement at Customer's instruction.

2.5 Training Opt-Out: Customers may opt out of model training on their
    data via API configuration (zero data retention mode available for API).
    ChatGPT Enterprise: training on Enterprise data is OFF by default.

    [CARLSBERG LEGAL NOTE — POSITIVE]: Zero data retention (ZDR) mode
    for API usage eliminates the model training concern. Must be contractually
    confirmed and technically verified. Add to IT Security checklist.

2.6 Personal Data Breach Notification: OpenAI will notify Customer
    "without undue delay" after becoming aware of a Personal Data Breach.

    [CARLSBERG LEGAL NOTE — MEDIUM RISK]: "Without undue delay" is weaker
    than Carlsberg's standard 24-hour breach notification requirement.
    GDPR Article 33 requires notification to supervisory authority within
    72 hours — OpenAI's obligation to notify Customer must precede this.
    Recommend: negotiate a specific 24-hour SLA for breach notification.

3. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY RIGHTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

3.1 Output Ownership: As between Customer and OpenAI, Customer owns the
    Output generated by the Services (to the extent permitted by applicable law).
    OpenAI assigns all rights, title, and interest in Output to Customer.

    [CARLSBERG LEGAL NOTE — POSITIVE]: Output ownership is clearly assigned
    to Carlsberg. This is significantly better than many AI vendors.
    Fine-tuned model outputs using Carlsberg data are Customer-owned.

3.2 Input Ownership: Customer retains ownership of all Input provided to
    the Services.

    [CARLSBERG LEGAL NOTE — POSITIVE]: No IP transfer of Carlsberg's
    proprietary data, recipes, or business information submitted as Input.

3.3 Similarity of Content: Due to the nature of AI, Output may not be unique.
    Other users may receive similar Output. Assignment does not extend to
    third-party Output.

    [CARLSBERG LEGAL NOTE — MEDIUM RISK]: Non-uniqueness of Output creates
    risk for Carlsberg using AI-generated content in marketing, product
    development, or competitive contexts. Must be communicated to business
    units. Recommend: do not use AI Output without human review for
    externally-published content.

3.4 Model Training: If Customer opts out of model training (see 2.5),
    Input will not be used to train OpenAI's foundation models.

3.5 Prohibited Uses: Customer may not use Services to:
    (a) Develop models that compete with OpenAI.
    (b) Reverse-engineer OpenAI's models, algorithms, or systems.
    (c) Automatically extract Output at scale without authorisation.

    [CARLSBERG LEGAL NOTE — LOW RISK]: Standard restrictions. Carlsberg's
    intended use case (demand forecasting, internal analytics) does not
    conflict with these restrictions.

4. SECURITY AND COMPLIANCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.1 OpenAI maintains the following certifications and compliance frameworks:
    - SOC 2 Type II (annual audit, report available under NDA)
    - ISO 27001 (certified)
    - GDPR compliant (as Data Processor)
    - CCPA compliant
    - CSA STAR Level 1

    [CARLSBERG LEGAL NOTE — POSITIVE]: Strong security posture. SOC 2 Type II
    and ISO 27001 are Carlsberg's minimum requirements for Tier 1 vendors.
    Request SOC 2 report under NDA as part of due diligence.

4.2 Penetration Testing: OpenAI conducts annual third-party penetration tests.
    Summary results available on request under NDA.

4.3 Encryption: All data encrypted in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest (AES-256).

4.4 Access Controls: Multi-factor authentication enforced for all OpenAI
    internal access to Customer Data systems.

4.5 Uptime SLA: OpenAI API targets 99.9% monthly uptime.
    ChatGPT Enterprise: 99.9% uptime SLA with credits for downtime.

    [CARLSBERG LEGAL NOTE — POSITIVE]: 99.9% uptime with credits is
    acceptable for non-critical internal tooling. Not recommended for
    production-critical supply chain systems without fallback.

4.6 Security Incident Response: OpenAI maintains a documented incident
    response process. P1 incidents (service-wide): response within 1 hour.

5. COMMERCIAL TERMS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.1 Pricing Model: Usage-based (per token) for API access.
    ChatGPT Enterprise: Annual subscription, volume pricing available.

5.2 Enterprise Pricing: Enterprise pricing available with volume commitment.
    Dedicated account management included at Enterprise tier.

5.3 Price Changes: OpenAI may change prices with minimum 30 days notice.
    Subscriptions: price changes take effect at next renewal.

    [CARLSBERG LEGAL NOTE — MEDIUM RISK]: 30-day notice for price changes
    is shorter than Carlsberg's preferred 90-day notice for material price
    increases. Negotiate: request 90-day notice for increases >10%.

5.4 Limitation of Liability: OpenAI's aggregate liability is capped at:
    (a) Fees paid in the 12 months preceding the claim; OR
    (b) USD 100 (for consumer use cases).

    [CARLSBERG LEGAL NOTE — HIGH RISK]: The USD $100 cap applies to
    non-enterprise consumer terms. For Enterprise agreements, the 12-month
    fees cap applies — which is the relevant provision for Carlsberg.
    However, at scale API usage, this cap may be insufficient for a
    significant data breach or service failure affecting Carlsberg's
    supply chain operations. Recommend: negotiate minimum liability cap
    of EUR 5M or 24-month fees, whichever is greater.

5.5 Warranty Disclaimer: Services provided "AS IS." No warranties of
    merchantability, fitness for purpose, or accuracy of Output.

    [CARLSBERG LEGAL NOTE — MEDIUM RISK]: Standard AI vendor disclaimer.
    Carlsberg must implement human-in-the-loop review for all
    business-critical decisions based on AI Output. Document this
    in internal AI governance policy.

5.6 Dedicated CSM: Enterprise tier includes dedicated Customer Success Manager
    and Technical Account Manager (TAM).

5.7 SLA Credits: Service credits issued for downtime below SLA threshold.
    Credits applied to next invoice (not cash refunds).

6. TERMINATION AND EXIT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

6.1 Customer may terminate for convenience with 30 days written notice
    (subscription: at renewal).

6.2 Data Export: Following termination, Customer Data will be deleted within
    30 days of termination, or returned in machine-readable format on request.

    [CARLSBERG LEGAL NOTE — MEDIUM RISK]: 30-day deletion window is acceptable
    but must be confirmed technically. Carlsberg must ensure internal data
    exports are completed before termination for any fine-tuned models or
    stored embeddings. Exit plan required as part of vendor onboarding.

6.3 OpenAI may terminate or suspend access for:
    (a) Breach of Terms or Usage Policies
    (b) Legal compliance
    (c) Risk of harm to OpenAI, users, or third parties

    [CARLSBERG LEGAL NOTE — MEDIUM RISK]: Unilateral suspension right is
    broad. For production-critical workloads, this creates business continuity
    risk. Recommend: ensure no single-vendor dependency for critical AI
    infrastructure.

7. BUSINESS CONTINUITY AND VENDOR VIABILITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

7.1 OpenAI is a Delaware-incorporated company with:
    - Revenue: >$3.7B ARR (2024, public reporting)
    - Funding: $6.6B raised (Series E, Oct 2024, lead: Thrive Capital)
    - Valuation: ~$157B (Dec 2024)
    - Employees: ~3,500+
    - Key investors: Microsoft (49% economic interest), SoftBank, Sequoia

    [CARLSBERG LEGAL NOTE — POSITIVE]: Strong financial position. Not a
    viability risk in the traditional sense. Microsoft's deep integration
    (Azure infrastructure, Copilot) provides additional continuity assurance.

7.2 Concentration Risk: OpenAI controls ~60% of enterprise LLM market.
    Key-person dependency: CEO Sam Altman (historical governance instability
    in Nov 2023 noted — resolved, but board composition remains a risk factor).

    [CARLSBERG LEGAL NOTE — MEDIUM RISK]: Market concentration and key-person
    risk noted. Nov 2023 board crisis resolved but demonstrated governance
    fragility. Multi-vendor AI strategy recommended (e.g. OpenAI + Anthropic
    or Azure OpenAI as fallback).
